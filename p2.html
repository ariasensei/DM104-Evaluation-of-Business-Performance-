Core Concepts Explained

1

What is Performance Measurement?

Simple Definition:

Performance measurement is the process of tracking and evaluating how well a business is doing at achieving its goals. It's like keeping score in a game – you need to know if you're winning, losing, or tied!

🏪 Real-World Example

A coffee shop manager tracks:
• Daily sales revenue (target: $1,500/day)
• Customer satisfaction (target: 4.5/5 stars)
• Wait time (target: under 5 minutes)

These measurements show if the business is operating well.

Why It Matters:

  • ✓ Know what's working and what isn't
  • ✓ Make better business decisions
  • ✓ Set clear goals for teams
  • ✓ Track progress over time
  • ✓ Identify problems early
2

Strategy & Performance Measurement

Connection:

Your business strategy is your big plan for success. Performance measurement checks if your plan is working. They work together like a map and a GPS – the map is your strategy, and performance measurement is the GPS telling you if you're on the right route.

🏥 Healthcare Example

Strategy: "Provide the best patient care in the city"

Performance measures:
• Patient recovery time
• Patient satisfaction scores
• Doctor availability
• Treatment success rates

These show if they're actually achieving their strategy.

The Connection Flow:

📍 Strategy: What we want to achieve
↓
📊 Measures: Specific things to track
↓
📈 Data: Collect the numbers
↓
✅ Decision: Adjust if needed
3

Role of Information Systems & Analytics

What are they?

Information Systems (IS): The computer systems that collect and store business data

Analytics: The tools and techniques to analyze that data and find useful insights

📱 Social Media Platform

IS: Servers that store user posts, likes, shares

Analytics: Tools showing:
• Most trending topics
• User engagement rates
• Peak usage times

These help the platform make better decisions about features.

🛒 E-commerce Store

IS: Website database tracking purchases, browsing

Analytics: Reports on:
• Best-selling products
• Customer buying patterns
• Conversion rates

Helps optimize inventory and marketing.

🏢 Manufacturing Plant

IS: Sensors on machines tracking production

Analytics: Dashboards showing:
• Production speed
• Quality issues
• Equipment downtime

Enables quick problem-solving.

Interactive Activities

👤

Individual Activity: Identify Performance Measures

📋 Your Task:

Choose ONE of these organizations and identify 5 performance measures that would help them achieve their strategy:

🎓 University

Goal: Provide quality education and prepare students for careers

🏥 Hospital

Goal: Deliver excellent healthcare and save lives

🍽️ Restaurant

Goal: Serve great food quickly and build loyal customers

🛍️ Retail Store

Goal: Maximize sales and improve customer experience

💡 Tip: Make sure each measure connects to the organization's strategy!

👥

Group Activity: Strategy Debate

👥 Group Discussion (3-4 students):

You are consulting for a mid-sized tech company. Half your group believes the company should focus on product quality, while the other half wants to focus on market expansion. Discuss and decide which is more important, and what performance measures would support each strategy.

🎯 Team A: Quality Focus

Strategy: Build the best product on the market

Potential Measures:
• Bug reports per release
• Customer satisfaction rating
• Product reliability uptime
• Customer retention rate
• Returns/complaints ratio

🚀 Team B: Expansion Focus

Strategy: Grow to new markets quickly

Potential Measures:
• Market share growth (%)
• New customer acquisition rate
• Revenue growth rate
• Sales team performance
• Geographic expansion milestones

💡 Tip: Consider both short-term and long-term implications!

Key Takeaways

  • ✓ Performance measurement helps organizations track progress toward goals and make better decisions
  • ✓ Strategy and measurement are connected: Measures tell you if your strategy is working
  • ✓ Information Systems collect and store the data we need to measure
  • ✓ Analytics helps us understand what the data means and make insights
  • ✓ Good measures are connected to strategy and actionable for decision-making
